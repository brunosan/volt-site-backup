{ websites }

Katal Center for Health, Equity and Justice

{ Visit site }

The Katal Center for Health, Equity and Justice is a non-profit that deals largely with ending mass criminalization, mass incarceration, and ending the war on drugs. They came to me wanting a bolder, fresher look for their website. I combined black and white images with a bright color palette, and gave them a simple update to their logo with white text encircled in a rich blue. The circle motif is repeated on various elements throughout the site, creating a cohesive look. They also wanted their site to act as a resource. For this I made the “Issues” front and center, and added related content to every issue page.

Santa Ana Building Healthy Communities

{ Visit site }

Santa Ana Building Healthy Communities is a non-profit dedicated to promoting health, education, and equity for the people of Santa Ana, California. Their community is predominantly Mexican. Inspired by the bright color palette seen in Mexican art, I combined blue, orange, green, and pink for a dramatic look. Coupled with this is a modern slanted design element throughout the site for added impact.

Education Foundation of Eagle County

{ Visit site }

The Education Foundation of Eagle County in Colorado first came to me wanting a splash page to promote an upcoming fundraising drive. The page was quite text-heavy, with a lot of quotes. I decided to use an interesting origami shape as a type of quote bubble for each quote. The page was so successful that they decided to turn it into their main website. I utilized the quote bubbles as a sidebar on the other site pages. I also added a slanted button styling, playing on the origami shape of the bubbles.

The Food Shed Co-op

{ Visit site }

The people behind the Food Shed are trying to bring a new food co-op to McHenry County, Illinois, and are using the NationBuilder platform get support and sell shares in the company. They had a fantastic branding schema already in place and my job was to create a website that complements their earthy style. I also streamlined the share purchase process to make it much simpler for visitors.

North Brooklyn Angels

{ Visit site }

The North Brooklyn Angels are a non-profit that is taking the issue of hunger, poverty, and homelessness to the street. They have a mobile soup kitchen that parks in different spots around Brooklyn to feed those in need. To complement their rebel-with-a-cause style logo, I used a black, white, and red color palette, asymmetrical boxes for their statistics, and Brooklyn's famous water towers as a backdrop. A custom map with the soup kitchen food truck locations and times of service was also included.

Friends of the Palo Alto Children's Theatre

{ Visit site }

For the Friends of the Palo Alto Children's Theatre's (FOPACT) website, I wanted to create a site that was informational, yet also reflected the fun nature of this children's theatre in Silicone Valley. Combining big, bold images of theatre productions, a lively color palate, and marquee-style fonts, the website provides parents with the info they need and entices kids to be a part of this great organization.
